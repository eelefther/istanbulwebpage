Pierre Lotti Hill and cable car

Pierre Loti Hill is a hill located in Eyüp district of Istanbul. It is a famous tourist attraction in Istanbul, as it offers a panoramic view of the Golden Horn and the city. The hill is named after the French writer Pierre Loti, who lived in Istanbul in the late 19th century and wrote about the city in his novels. There is a café at the top of the hill where visitors can enjoy a cup of Turkish tea or coffee while taking in the view.

To reach Pierre Loti Hill, visitors can take a cable car from the Eyüp Sultan Mosque. The cable car ride is a short and scenic journey that takes visitors up the hill in just a few minutes. Once at the top, visitors can explore the hilltop café, take in the view, and visit the nearby Eyüp Sultan Mosque.

Pierre Loti Hill is a popular spot for both locals and tourists, and is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of Istanbul. The hill is especially beautiful at sunset, when the city is bathed in golden light and the view is truly breathtaking.
