Spice Bazaar

The Spice Bazaar is one of the oldest bazaars in Istanbul, located in the Eminönü quarter of the Fatih district. It is the second largest covered shopping complex after the Grand Bazaar. The Spice Bazaar is the place to go for spices, dried fruits, nuts, seeds, and Turkish delights. The bazaar was built in 1660 and is still one of the most popular shopping destinations in Istanbul.

It is also known as the Egyptian Bazaar, as it was built with the revenues from the Ottoman eyalet of Egypt in 1660. The bazaar is a part of the Yeni Mosque complex, which is located right next to it. The Spice Bazaar is a must-visit for anyone who wants to experience the sights, sounds, and smells of Istanbul.

It is a great place to buy spices, dried fruits, nuts, seeds, and Turkish delights. The bazaar is also home to a number of shops selling souvenirs, textiles, and other goods. The Spice Bazaar is a great place to explore and experience the sights, sounds, and smells of Istanbul.
