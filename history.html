Ancient Times

660 BCE: Greek settlers, led by Byzas, established Byzantion (Byzantium) on the European side of the Bosporus, taking advantage of its strategic location for trade and defense.

73 CE: The city came under Roman control, integrating into the vast Roman Empire.

Roman Period

330 CE: Emperor Constantine I renamed the city Constantinople and declared it the capital of the Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine Empire). It was referred to as the "New Rome."

During this period, Constantinople flourished as a political, cultural, and economic hub, with significant developments like the construction of the Hagia Sophia (537 CE).

Byzantine Period

1204: During the Fourth Crusade, Constantinople was sacked, leading to the establishment of the short-lived Latin Empire (1204–1261).

1261: The Byzantine Empire regained control under the Palaiologos dynasty, but the city struggled with economic and political instability.

Ottoman Period

1453: The city was captured by Sultan Mehmed II (Mehmed the Conqueror) after a 53-day siege. This marked the end of the Byzantine Empire and the beginning of Ottoman rule. The city became the capital of the Ottoman Empire and was renamed Istanbul.

The Ottomans transformed the city with stunning architectural works such as the Topkapi Palace, Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque), and Grand Bazaar.

Türkiye

1923: With the foundation of the Turkish Republic by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the capital was moved to Ankara. Despite this, Istanbul remained Turkey’s largest city and its economic and cultural powerhouse.

Throughout the 20th century, Istanbul underwent rapid industrialization and urbanization. It became a bridge between the modern and the historic, connecting Asia and Europe.

Today, Istanbul is a vibrant metropolis of over 15 million people. Its historical heritage, including landmarks like the Hagia Sophia, Topkapi Palace, and the Bosphorus, attracts millions of visitors annually. As a unique city spanning two continents, Istanbul continues to thrive as a global cultural and economic hub.
